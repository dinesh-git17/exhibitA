Design Direction
Do / Don't: Tone & Color Treatment
Guiding principles for every dark-mode design decision in Exhibit A.
Do
Use warm-tinted near-blacks with rosewood or umber undertones. Every dark surface should feel warm, like a room with wooden walls. Proposed base: #16120F.
Don't
Use pure black (#000000) or cool-tinted dark grays. Pure black is harsh on OLED. Cool grays feel clinical. This app is intimate, not industrial.
Do
Keep burgundy in the garnet/rosewood range when lifting for dark mode. Target: deep enough to feel wine-dark, light enough for readability. Proposed: #A8405A.
Don't
Let burgundy drift to medium pink or hot pink. Pink reads as playful or juvenile. The app's tone is romantic authority, not Valentine's Day card. Current dark lift #C4526A is too pink.
Do
Use luminance-based elevation in dark mode: lighter surfaces for raised elements. Supplement with 1px warm-glow borders at very low opacity. Shadows are invisible on dark backgrounds.
Don't
Rely on the same shadow stack that works in light mode. rgba(44,33,24,0.06) on a #1A1614 background is literally invisible.
Do
Treat gold as a ceremonial accent. Signature rules, decorative dividers, and the "Est. 2025" date should gleam. Gold should feel like gold leaf, not beige.
Don't
Let gold wash out to pale yellow. On dark backgrounds, undersaturated gold loses its preciousness. It becomes another shade of tan. Re-saturate: #D4B060.
Do
Use the reading surface for all reading content. Letters, thoughts, and contract pages should use bg.reading + paper noise. Reading is a distinct mode.
Don't
Put reading content on the app chrome background. Letters and thoughts currently use bg.primary. The reading experience deserves its own warmer, textured surface.
Guiding Metaphor
The same book, read by candlelight.
Light mode is afternoon light in a favorite library. Dark mode is evening reading by lamplight. The book doesn't change. The light does. Everything should feel warmer, closer, more intimate -- not darker, flatter, or colder.
